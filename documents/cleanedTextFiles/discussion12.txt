The best advice I can give is to tailor each resume and cover letter you make to the position you are applying for. I can not begin to tell you how many cover letters I have read that are a general template for applying for any job. We disregard those candidates almost immediately. Mention how your past experience will help you to be successful at the job you are applying for. Keep your cover letter to a respectful paragraph, and do not let your resume go over one page.

if you can find any information about people that are hiring and reach out to them directly that's really helpful. When I hire student workers we literally get so many applications and it's really hard to trim it down when they all basically have the same generic resume and cover letter. I'm in a somewhat similar position to yours, really needing to secure myself with a part-time job on-campus or at least nearby campus first so I can quit my full-time job that is taking up so much of my time and energy for school work, without having to worry about falling behind on bills.

Out of all the employers, most ASU departments have been absolutely the most selective ones in my experience even after changing up my resume and cover letters to tailor to each specific position.

I can understand many ASU departments being selective of candidates for higher level student worker jobs but it's pretty absurd for them to be incredibly selective of candidates for even student worker I jobs considering those ones are entry-level/no-experience type of jobs.

ASU has been the only employer in my experience, so far that absolutely requires you to have a cover letter submitted for any job position on their application, while all other jobs I've applied for, makes cover letter submissions optional.
