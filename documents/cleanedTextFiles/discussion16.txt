Do ASU software engineering master's students get TA/RA/GA opportunities or is it just for MS CS students?
Assistant
Short answer: Yes—Arizona State University (ASU) offers TA/RA/GA opportunities to students in the MS in Software Engineering, but availability, eligibility rules, and competitiveness differ from the MSCS program. Funding is more limited in professional or industry-oriented programs than in research-heavy CS degrees.

Key points and practical guidance

Types of positions
Graduate Teaching Assistant (GTA/TA): teaching-related roles (grading, labs, recitations, guest lecturing). Managed by academic units and typically open to graduate students across related programs, including Software Engineering.
Graduate Research Assistant (GRA/RA): research-funded positions tied to faculty grants/projects. Available when faculty in software engineering or allied labs have grant funding and accept SE students onto their teams.
Graduate Assistant (GA)/Administrative: non-teaching staff roles supporting departments, labs, or centers. Often advertised centrally and can be held by any eligible graduate student.
Eligibility and prioritization
Must be a degree-seeking graduate student in good academic standing and meet enrollment requirements (usually minimum credit hours).
International students need valid F-1/J-1 work authorization compliance (on-campus employment rules apply).
Departments often prioritize PhD and MSCS students for research-funded GRAs because those programs are explicitly research-oriented. MS Software Engineering (especially professional or online tracks) may be secondary priority for limited GA/RA funding.
GTAs for large undergraduate courses (programming, data structures) are commonly filled by CS and SE students; faculty and course coordinators decide hires.
How competitive and where it’s concentrated
More RA opportunities in research groups: AI, systems, security, software engineering research labs. If you want RA work, target SE faculty with active grants.
On-campus GTA and GA roles are distributed across Tempe, Polytechnic, Downtown Phoenix, and West campuses depending on where the program and courses are offered.
Part-time, hourly TA/GA roles may be more accessible than full stipend GRAs.
How to increase chances
Contact faculty whose research aligns with your interests and express willingness to join as an RA; include concise evidence of relevant skills (GitHub, publications, course projects).
Build TA-relevant experience: past tutoring, grading, lab assistance, or strong course performance in core CS/SE classes.
Apply early each semester to posted GTA/GA openings on the department and university job sites; monitor course coordinator announcements.
Network with instructors and graduate coordinators; ask about upcoming grant-funded projects or openings.
Maintain a strong GPA and meet required enrollment/work-authorization rules for eligibility.
Typical funding levels and workload
GTA/GRA appointments range from partial tuition support plus stipend to hourly wages; level and hours vary (commonly 10–20 hours/week).
Full funding is more common for PhD and research MS students; SE master’s students should expect mixed outcomes—some secure part-time support, fewer get full-funded RA roles.
Where to find openings
ASU School of Computing, Informatics, and Decision Systems Engineering (CIDSE) and Ira A. Fulton Schools of Engineering job boards.
Individual faculty web pages and lab sites.
ASU Handshake and university HR postings for student employment.
Departmental graduate coordinator or program director announcements.
Examples (typical stories)

A SE MS student receives a GTA position grading assignments for introductory programming after applying through the department; works 10–15 hours/week and receives a stipend plus partial tuition reduction.
A SE student joins a software engineering research lab led by a professor with an NSF grant; becomes an RA working on empirical software engineering experiments while receiving a stipend tied to the grant.
A fully online/professional-track SE student finds fewer on-campus RA opportunities but obtains an adjunct GA role supporting course materials and forums remotely.
Bottom line: MS Software Engineering students at ASU are eligible for TA/RA/GA roles, but opportunities are fewer and often more competitive compared with MSCS/PhD students. Proactivity—contacting faculty, applying early, demonstrating relevant skills—substantially improves chances.
