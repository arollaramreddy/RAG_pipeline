I’d like to answer as someone who volunteers at an ASU career fair every year. If you are not getting what you want from a career fair, maybe you are expecting the wrong thing. Maybe some places hire straight from career fairs, but most professional places don’t. You are just one of 60 people we are going to talk to that day and in the 10 minutes I spend with you, I have no idea what you are capable of, what you know or what you are like. What I can do in that 10 minutes is explain what we do, what we look for and, crucially, what our hiring and interview process is like. I can touch on our expectations and find out if you can hold a conversation, but honestly, if you apply and make it through HR to an interview, what you gain from the career fair is that we have already met and maybe you won’t be a nervous wreck when selling yourself to the interview team. A career fair is not a place to find a job, it is a place to find a little leg up on your competition. Spending time with me learning our interview questions (spoiler: most of the time they are the same) or learning what we want and if it aligns with what you want (what we want won’t change, if you want something different, it’s not gonna change what we want) and talking to different employers about their shared needs are what you should expect from a career fair. We wouldn’t even be allowed to hire somebody directly from a career fair and even if I could, I wouldn’t. That said, almost all of our interns spoke with me at a career fair but I didn’t remember any of them.
