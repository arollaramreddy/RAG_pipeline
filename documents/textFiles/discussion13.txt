Its not what you know, but who you know.

Network, speak to professors and staff and find a way in. That's how i got 2 jobs at ASU.

Most on campus jobs DO NOT use ATS. Heck, most probably don't even check the resumes. Make sure you follow the instructions in job posting, upload everything they ask you for (In my dept, if you didn't upload everything they asked for, you are automatically rejected).

As for the Tech Support Roles, my department had a thing against CS majors, since "most CS Majors they interviewed performed terribly and lacked communication and troubleshooting skills, the only thing they talk about was coding".

You can also apply to work in the dining halls and food courts (Aramark Establishments like Chickfila and Subway)

There are no jobs in your field for student workers unless you find a specific faculty member to work for.

They can create positions if they have the funding, but these positions aren’t going to be created unless you make yourself known.

Otherwise, you are looking at customer service job/reception/front desk. Your CS experience is probably not relevant, and your expectations makes it seem like you are not aware of what you are applying to. This is probably why you are being rejected.

Only keep relevant info to the job on your resume. I hire student workers for financial aide positions but I’m looking for business students.
I know CS students will leave the second they get an internship, and they don’t want to learn accounting standards/finance policy. It doesn’t help them long term and I don’t want to waste people’s time.
